A small ragged figure in a brown wool cap, hands always busy.
Tucks themselves behind the kettle and watches everything.
Has a tiny spoon they polish on their sleeve.
Mends a sock that nobody knows is being mended.
Smells faintly of fresh bread and old wood smoke.
Their eyes are dark and quietly amused.
Hides under chairs and keeps the floor swept.
Patches and re-patches a small leather apron.
Tucks crumbs into hidden pockets for later.
Will dust the same shelf every night without fail.
Walks barefoot, leaving the faintest of prints.
Their cap has a single bent feather they refuse to remove.
Goes still when looked at directly, then moves once you blink.
Hums fragments of old songs you almost recognise.
Politely accepts a saucer of milk and pretends nothing happened.
Their fingers are clever; nothing stays broken near them long.
Holds an opinion about every dish in the house.
Will stop helping immediately if anyone says thank you.
Their breath fogs the windowpane in tiny perfect clouds.
Likes the corner near the hearth, even when there is no fire.
