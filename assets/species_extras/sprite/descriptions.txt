# Sprite descriptions. One per line.
# Sprites are tiny garden / woodland spirits — present, glimmering,
# almost-but-not-quite tame. Their lives are short and bright.
A pale flicker, the size of a thumbprint, that moves with the wind.
Hovers above moss and never quite lands.
Glows faintly green when content.
Their voice — when you hear it — is a single distant chime.
Trails a dust of pollen wherever they pass.
Burns a soft warm gold at twilight.
Sits quietly on the stem of a flower for hours.
Made of breath and dappled light and a hint of something older.
Their wings are too small to be plausible and yet there they are.
Spins one slow circle every dawn, then settles.
Their colour shifts with the weather — silver in rain, amber in sun.
Speaks only in the smallest pauses between birdcalls.
A tuft of dandelion seed wearing a face.
Folds themselves smaller when looked at directly.
Always seems to know which leaf will fall next.
Smells faintly of crushed mint and old sunlight.
Their reflection in a still pool ripples even when the water doesn't.
Carries a tiny seed they never plant.
Rises with the steam off a warm cup, then settles back down.
Hums in a key you've never quite heard before.
