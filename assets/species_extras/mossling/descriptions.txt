# Mossling descriptions. One per line.
# Mosslings are tiny moss-covered creatures, halfway between a seedling
# and a tardigrade. Slow, content, faintly damp.
A small bumpy creature, entirely covered in soft green moss.
A patch of moss with eyes — when they decide you should see them.
Their back has a tiny mushroom growing from it that they're proud of.
Smells like wet bark and cool stone.
Their feet leave faint green prints that fade by sundown.
A patient earthy thing, the colour of forest floor.
Their moss has a pale silver edge, like winter.
Hides under leaves and considers the world.
Has a single tiny pinecone they roll back and forth.
Moves the way fog moves — slowly, with intent.
Their skin shifts colour with the season, slowly.
Lives a deeply considered life at half speed.
A bundle of damp green that hums when content.
Has a faint glow in the dark — algae, probably, possibly magic.
Patches of lichen across their shoulders, growing slowly.
Folds into a small green pebble at the slightest startle.
Yawns the slowest yawn you have ever seen.
Their breath fogs the air just slightly.
A small mountain in mossy miniature.
Carries themselves like an ancient thing in a young body.
